A. P. J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India's missile and space technology programs. His contributions to India's defense capabilities, including the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him widespread admiration. Beyond his scientific endeavors, Kalam was a passionate advocate for education, particularly among India's youth, emphasizing the importance of innovation, creativity, and moral leadership. His humility, dedication, and inspirational speeches continue to inspire millions worldwide.
